Engineering

Engineering is the application of scientific and mathematical principles to design, develop, and improve structures, systems,
and technologies. It encompasses a wide range of disciplines such as Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, and Computer Science Engineering.
Engineers play a vital role in solving real-world problems and driving innovation across industries.
The field is essential for infrastructure development, technological advancement, and improving quality of life.

Degree

A Bachelor's degree in Engineering (B.E. or B.Tech) is a four-year undergraduate program that provides
foundational knowledge and practical skills in a specific engineering field. Students study core subjects, undertake lab work, and engage in projects
to understand how theoretical concepts apply to real-world scenarios. The degree equips graduates with the technical expertise needed for entry-level jobs in engineering and technology sectors.
It also lays the groundwork for higher studies and professional certifications

Post graduation

Post-graduation in engineering, such as M.E., M.Tech, or M.S., is a two-year advanced program pursued after completing a bachelor's degree.
It allows students to specialize in a specific area like Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, VLSI, or Structural Engineering. This level of study involves in-depth coursework, research,
and often a thesis or project. A postgraduate
degree enhances career prospects by qualifying individuals for higher-level roles in research, academia, and specialized industry positions.
